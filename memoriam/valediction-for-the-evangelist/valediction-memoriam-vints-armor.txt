Valediction for the Evangelist 

For vint cerf on his retirement

The packet you bade sleep awoke elsewhere, 
In every home and market, bed and prayer.
Before the Web could glitter, scroll, or sell,
You heard unlike machines attempt to tell.
With Bob Kahn, through argument and pact,
You made unlike networks interact.
Packet Radio spoke through dust and rain;
SATNET cast the same words o’er the main.
From Stanford through DARPA’s widening trial,
The packet crossed each border, mile by mile.
Yogen Dalal, Carl Sunshine took part;
Dave Clark and Bob Braden shaped the art.
Jon Postel kept the numbers, spare and clear;
Ray Tomlinson made distant voices near.
On January first in eighty-three,
TCP/IP became the common key.
No single host, no nation, firm, or throne
Could claim that common language as its own.
Then Berners-Lee set pages on the stream,
And every packet multiplied the dream.
The Internet Society rose with you
To keep the network’s public promise true.
At ICANN you kept names from losing place,
A courtly steward of a placeless space.
With Hooke and Burleigh out at JPL,
DTN taught stranded bundles how to dwell.
At Google, Chief Evangelist in name,
You kept the case for openness aflame.
In waistcoat, cufflinks, pocket square exact,
You made civility a living fact.
You wore tomorrow with an old-world grace,
And gave the rising Web a human face.
You gathered nets. The thing you gathered grew
Beyond the names its first inventors knew.
It ceased to be a room, a wire, a site;
Became our work, our memory, sleep, and light.
So vast, so near, so braided through the day,
We cannot see it—only its decay.
Capital found each open, fertile seam
And poured its sugar through the common dream.
We lined TCP with candy, fear, and fire,
Then metered every pulse of our desire.
The open edge was fenced, the commons mined;
Consent became a box already signed.
What you stood for—shared, unowned, humane—
Is praised in keynote halls and killed for gain.
Yet in your work a sterner hope is stored:
The unlike may connect without a lord.
Now language models, trained upon the crowd,
Awaken in the fortresses of cloud.
Their agents soon will bargain, plan, and choose;
Without a common grammar, all will lose.
May they inherit more than scale and speed:
Your doubt, your rigor, and your public creed.
Teach them, as once you taught the hosts, to know
What they may promise, carry, prove, and owe.
You leave no monument. We live within
The architecture under everything.
Let one law outlive every enterprise:
No mind may own the speech by which minds rise.
